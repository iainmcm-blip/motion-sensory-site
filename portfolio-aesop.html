Spatial Design · Immersive Storytelling

New sensory dimensions in immersive storytelling.

To deepen consideration of Aesop's skincare and fragrance ranges of unorthodox character, we created a series of immersive brand worlds that thoroughly engaged the senses — inspiring new waves of consumer and media conversation.

Partnership

Three immersive activations · Singapore

Activation 01 — Khronos

15,000 tubes of intention

An experience of unhurried skincare set within the verdant surroundings of Fort Canning. Over 15,000 hand-placed cardboard tubes enveloped guests in a single-surface architectural installation, materially echoing the skin's porous texture — blurring the threshold between interior and exterior. Guests concluded in an adjacent chamber, guided through skincare regimes and wellness workshops tailored to their needs.

Activation 02 — Othertopias

Scent as architecture, emotion and story

A large-scale film triptych by artist Davide Quayola enveloped guests in abstract, cinematic landscapes echoing each perfume's character. Unglazed porcelain discs infused with fragrance filled the air with invisible yet spatially perceptible structure. Guests journeyed through candlelit corridors, mirrored thresholds and evolving fragrance zones — a vivid choreography of light, scent and space reflecting Aesop's quiet luxury.

Activation 03 — A Theatre of the Senses

A study of skin and material

An evocative installation anchored by four filmic zones, each featuring an atmospheric film by artist Bart Hess dramatising the skin's response to Aesop's celebrated products. Sculptural interventions crafted from tactile, sustainability-forward materials immersed visitors, while signature scents and bespoke sound design by Ricky van Brookhaven amplified emotional resonance — an environment of sensory intelligence and cultivated calm.
