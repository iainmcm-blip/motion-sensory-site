The business case for multisensory alignment.

By Juliana Mattar 15 Min Read March 24, 2026

In the current landscape of digital-first interactions, the physical experience has become a rarity. This scarcity has transformed tactile and sensory touchpoints from functional requirements into high-value brand differentiators. When sound, scent, and visual rhythm align, they create a 'liminal resonance' that binds a consumer to a brand on a neurobiological level.

Modern brands often suffer from sensory fragmentation. A luxury hotel may feature world-class architecture, yet be undermined by a generic audio atmosphere or an uncurated olfactory profile. Multisensory alignment is the process of synchronizing these disparate elements to forge a unified, atmospheric identity. When every sensory cue tells the same story, the brand feels intentional. Customers recognise and remember it faster.

"When every sense is engaged, your brand becomes more than just a memory of an interaction. It becomes a resonance, a feeling, an inspiration that people carry with them."

— Juliana Mattar, Chief Sensory Officer

Designing through the senses

Scent

The most primal trigger of memory and desire. Fragrance has the power to shift perception and elevate brand loyalty.

Sound

Sonic landscapes echo culture and mood. Music is more than background — it is the pulse of a space.

The Congruence Principle

Choosing a scent that suits the setting and products reinforces the brand and strengthens connection. When a scent matches the space and product, people feel more at ease and tend to see the brand as higher quality. Aligned senses create embodied emotion, the kind you don't just remember, you relive.
